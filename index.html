Rather than our glossy and colorful magazine format, manga are generally in black and white, and in a thicker, trade-paperback-like size. The books we get are usually several collected chapters that originally ran separately in popular Japanese magazines like Shonen Jump.

Shonen (for boys) - action/adventure, optimistic, Kid hero. Shojo (for girls) - romance and drama, lots of cute boys. The above two, especially shonen series, make up the vast majority of titles imported to the US, and are often marketed to an older age. Seinen (for men) - a lot more cynical, slower pace, more nuance, and probably more sexual or violent content. Josei (for women) - same as seinen in terms of tone, but with the shojo focus on relationships.
